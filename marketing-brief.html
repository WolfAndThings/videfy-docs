01 · Positioning

One-line
positioning statement.

Positioning
For creators and lean video teams who juggle six to twelve disconnected tools per project, Videfy holds the entire production in one workspace, from first idea to final cut. No more exporting between apps, losing versions, or rebuilding context every step. Unlike single-purpose tools (Runway for generation, Frame.io for review, CapCut for edit), Videfy is the layer they all sit inside.
Category: end-to-end video production workspace. Displaces: Adobe Creative Cloud + Frame.io + Runway / Pika / Luma + Loom + Dropbox + standalone caption tool. Buyer frame: "the stack we're already paying for, consolidated."
02 · Product

What Videfy
actually does.

Single workflow platform from solo creator to large team. One project carries every stage: storyboarding, AI generation (image, video, avatar, voice), multi-variant generation, scene-level editing, collaboration, review, render, native Adobe Premiere Pro handoff. Production layer that consolidates the stack.

Lead use case
Turn a rough idea into a video you can pitch, refine, and post, in one workspace, without leaving for another tool.
The arc: Idea → Feedback → Finished. One project. No handoffs between tools. Brief, refs, generated assets, edits, comments, approvals, exports all attached to the same storyboard.
Solo creator
1 person
Ships short-form, spec ads, talking-head, faceless content. Single project, single workflow, single bill.
Lean producer / team
3 to 20 people
Shared workspace. Per-editor budgets. Free unlimited client review. Editors keep their finishing tool of choice.
Large team / agency
20+ people, multi-client
Restricted workspaces (no cross-client contamination), SSO, shared usage pool, custom MCP, legal review available.

03 · Jobs to be done

Three jobs.
One platform answers all three.

JobSituation → motivation → outcome
Ship the idea When I have a rough idea and a deadline, I want to move it through structure, generation, review, and export without context-switching, so I can deliver a finished video this week instead of next.
Test the campaign When I have one concept and limited budget, I want to generate ten variants and pick what works, so I can spend on the winner instead of guessing on the brief.
Close the feedback loop When stakeholders or clients review a cut, I want their feedback on the timecode and in one thread, so I do not lose an hour reconciling Loom, Slack, and email into a revision list.

Honest read
Videfy wins on workflow-platform dimensions (memory, storyboard, multi-variant, review, MCP, honeypot). Videfy explicitly does not win on pro finishing. That lane belongs to Adobe / Resolve / Final Cut, and the Premiere extension exists to acknowledge that. The Creator tier faces real price pressure from Submagic / Captions / Opus Clip at $15 to $19/mo. Justify with storyboard + memory + multi-variant, not generation quality alone.

Honest watch-outs per avenue
Avenue 4 (Avatar): direct overlap with HeyGen and Synthesia. Wedge must be that the avatar lives inside a project (storyboard, multi-variant, review), not as a one-off render. If copy reduces to "record yourself without filming," it pattern-matches HeyGen. Lead with the workflow context. Avenue 5 (Premiere): a real share of pro / agency editors is on DaVinci Resolve or Final Cut Pro in 2026. Premiere-as-only-extension is a near-term wedge, not a long-term moat. Resolve extension belongs on the 12-month roadmap. Avenue 3 (Honeypot): credits-only generation must outperform free Runway and Pika previews on quality or it stalls at curiosity.

Hook stops the scroll

Reels land on a feature

Anti-drift filter · before any draft ships

Kill if
  • Sounds like Canva, VEED, Runway, CapCut, Descript, HeyGen
  • Uses "all-in-one" or "creative suite"
  • Uses "stunning, beautiful, 8K, hyperrealistic, perfect"
  • Leads with feature instead of outcome
  • Pigeonholes to "feedback only" or "AI magic only"
Keep if
  • Names the chaos before naming the solution
  • Describes a workflow outcome
  • Maps to a JTBD (§03), avenue (§08), or pain row (§05)
  • Solo operator or lean team is the protagonist
  • Hook stops the scroll on line one
