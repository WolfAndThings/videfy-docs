Production strategy
Video production
without the chaos.
Videfy is the platform for pain-free video production — together. One workspace that absorbs the tool sprawl living between idea and finished output, holds the project's memory, embeds AI inside the flow, lets clients review and approve without paying a seat tax, and gives you scene-level editing built right into the workflow. Not a pro post-production timeline. Not an AI generator. Not a creative suite. The cleaner production system around the tools people already use, with the collaboration, the memory, the embedded AI, and lightweight editing those tools never had in one place.

Two entry flows

Honeypot or
workspace. Two front doors.

Videfy has two entry states — not one. Pricing, product, and copy must support both. Confusing them is what kills funnels.

①
Flow 1 — Honeypot / instant-use
Anonymous · no signup
Value: exploration · curiosity · zero commitment until value is clear.
  • User lands via "Try for Free" CTA — no signup
  • Free roam: homepage, Story Creation, Start from Scratch, video tutorials
  • Click Generate → signup modal (email + password, no card)
  • Backend gates every generation endpoint with auth — popup is UX, security is server-side
  • Account created → Personal Workspace seeded + promo credits
  • Converts to Creator or Pro
②
Flow 2 — Workspace subscription
Recurring production system
Value: repeatability · organization · memory · production system · collaboration · client review · scale.
  • User enters with project intent (or has been invited to a workspace)
  • Creates project inside Personal or owned/joined workspace
  • Organizes assets, iterates, invites collaborators, invites clients to review
  • Buys top-ups for heavy actions
  • Upgrades tier as needs scale (Personal → Creator → Pro → Team)
Action tags inside the workflow
Every step in production should be tagged: included (in tier subscription) · metered (counts against monthly allowance) · top-up eligible (above-allowance use buys credits) · premium / future (advanced gen, packaging, when shipped). Without this tagging, pricing always feels bolted on later.
Upgrade moments inside the flow
These are not UX details — they are monetization handoff points. Surface them deliberately: "Want to save this project?" (Honeypot → account + Personal Workspace) · "Want to bring in a collaborator?" (Personal/Creator → Pro) · "Want a dedicated team workspace?" (Pro → Team) · "Want client review without limits?" (any tier — already free, lead with it) · "Want more iterations?" The "client review is free, unlimited, no account required" line gets reinforced often — it is the wedge.
Cost-sensitive operational notes
Flag the expensive moments in the flow so product + pricing stay aligned as usage grows: heavy model use (gen, avatar) · packaging / export compute · storage accumulation · repeated revisions · collaboration overhead. Each is a metered or top-up surface.

is not.

Knowing what Videfy is not is as important as knowing what it is. These lanes are already owned. Videfy stays out and stays clean.

The enemy

The real enemy
is the drag.

The drag is what every creator, producer, and team feels but can rarely name: the cumulative weight that makes every project heavier than it should be. Things take longer. Energy depletes by lunch. Momentum dies in the gaps between Slack and Drive and Frame.io. The work-against-the-work eats the actual work.

Locked enemy
Tool sprawl creates the drag. The four taxes are how the drag charges you.
What tool sprawl actually looks like
🗂
App sprawl
6–12 systems / project
Drives, chats, editors, generators, review tools, schedulers — all disconnected, all paying a tax at every handoff.
💸
Subscription sprawl
Monthly bill stack
Editor + AI generator + storage + collab + voiceover + transcription + scheduler. Each renews quietly. Total cost of "the workflow" is invisible.
🪙
Token / credit sprawl
Scattered AI spend
Runway here, ChatGPT there, MJ somewhere else. Spend fragmented. Every tool has its own meter.
💾
Asset & device sprawl
3 computers, 2 drives, 4 clouds
Footage and exports living everywhere except where you need them. The "right file" is on the laptop that isn't in the room.
🔑
Account & login sprawl
Different truth per tool
Every team member has different logins, different permissions, a different version of the truth across every tool.
💬
Client-review sprawl
5 channels, 5 stakeholders
Clients reviewing in Loom, in email replies, in Frame.io seats they never log into, in Slack DMs to the producer.
The five taxes the drag charges
📂
Operational tax
The visible one
File chaos, feedback chaos, handoff chaos. Time bleeds into ops. The surface layer teams already complain about.
🧠
Attention tax
The switching cost
Every tool jump = context reload. Flow state never starts. The cost of finding the work dwarfs the cost of doing it.
🎨
Creative tax
The energy drain
Producers chase status. Editors hunt files. Reviewers re-explain. The interesting decisions are what get cut.
🧬
Memory tax
The silent one
Every project starts cold. Lessons die in someone's head. No competitor carries this.
💸
Cost tax
The bill nobody adds up
Editor sub + AI gen sub + storage + collab tool + voiceover + transcription + scheduler + per-reviewer seat charges. Each line item small, total invisible. Plus switching costs, expired credits, double-paid transition months. "The way we work today" never shows up in one place.
What Videfy is, in one line
The platform that absorbs the drag — so the team stops managing the work and starts making it, together, with clients reviewing and approving for free.

How AI, automation, and memory work together — every stage

Three layers,
every stage.

The harder question is where Claude activates inside the production flow, what fires automatically without being asked, and what memory carries from one project into the next.

Three layers underneath every stage
Automation hooks — what fires without a prompt. AI in context — what Claude does because it understands the project. Memory carryover — what the next stage inherits without re-asking.
💡
Stage 01 — Idea
Project starts smart
  • Automation: project scaffolds itself from a brief — folders, version slots, brand context, deliverable type
  • AI in context: Claude inherits brand voice, prior reels, what worked, audience — proposes shot list, structure, references
  • Memory carryover: every prior project's lessons live inside the new one from minute one. The blank-page problem disappears.
💬
Stage 02 — Feedback
All notes, one place
  • Automation: comments from Slack paste, frame click, Loom transcript, voice note, client review link — collapse onto the work
  • AI in context: Claude summarizes, dedups, groups by clip & reviewer, flags conflicts, proposes a numbered revision plan
  • Memory carryover: what was approved, what was rejected, why — stored against the project. Reviewers stop re-explaining.
📦
Stage 03 — Finished
Handoff builds itself
  • Automation: handoff package builds itself — clips, takes, brief, feedback summary, export structure for CapCut / mobile / Premiere
  • AI in context: Claude writes "what to cut" summary, flags missing assets, surfaces unresolved feedback before export
  • Memory carryover: finished cut feeds back into project memory. The next reel starts smarter than the last.
✕
What Claude does not do
v1 scope discipline
  • Replace your pro post-production timeline — color, VFX, multitrack audio, advanced finishing belong in CapCut / Premiere / Resolve. Claude handles scene-level edits inside Videfy, then steps back when you go to finish.
  • Replace your creative judgment — Claude proposes, you decide
  • Operate outside your project — no off-topic chatbot mode
  • Push to platforms automatically — publishing stays in your hands
🔥
Why this is different from "AI for video"
Memory + tools = a real producer
Most "AI for video" products bolt a chatbot onto an editor. Videfy does the opposite — flow first, Claude inside via MCP, automation across stage transitions, and memory makes it useful past the first prompt. With Claude embedded — driving the tools, holding the context, packaging the handoff — it becomes a producer who already knows the project.

CapCut / mobile handoff — defined

A handoff,
not an integration.

Creators do final edits on mobile or in CapCut all the time. Videfy reduces the drag before that final mobile edit. The handoff is the product moment, not the editor.

✓
What it is
In scope for v1
A download / export option that bundles the right files and production context for editing in CapCut or on mobile.
  • Selected clips, takes, or sequence
  • Associated feedback and revision notes
  • Brief / brand context if attached
  • Export-ready file naming and structure
  • A simple summary of "what to cut"
✕
What it is not
Out of scope
Not deep CapCut integration. Not a CapCut competitor. Not a mobile editor inside Videfy.
  • No native CapCut SDK in v1
  • No mobile timeline
  • No mobile-side rendering
  • No replacing the user's editor of choice

and engineering need to answer.
