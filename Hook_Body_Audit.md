## Errors found (fix before Mon ship)

### 1. ManyChat trigger inconsistency ❌
Calendar uses **5 different triggers** across the week (FIRST / LAUNCH / GENERATE / DEMO / STORY). Confuses audience, splits ManyChat flows.

**Fix:** ONE trigger all week — `STUDIO` (ties to "you're the studio now" theme). Single ManyChat → routes by day to right asset (Mon → Prompt Pack · Tue-Wed → trial link · Thu → Generate · Fri → case study).

### 2. Day 1 hero hook generic ❌
"Big things tomorrow at 9 AM ET" — this is a yoga studio launch, not a tech product. No specificity, no curiosity.

### 3. Day 2 LinkedIn long-form too long ❌
800-1200 words. Industry data: LinkedIn long-form peaks at 600-800 words (after 1000 words → 50% drop in completion rate).

### 4. Day 2 hook contradicts our top patterns ❌
"AI video production not suck" — generic. Our 312K-engagement post used cost reframe. Apply same pattern.

### 5. Day 3 hook fails the data ❌
"How we built Videfy" — this generic SaaS hook has 0 hits in our top-10 viral patterns. None of our high-engagement posts use this format.

### 6. Day 6 recap hook ❌
"Wk 1 by the numbers" — used by every founder on X. Zero differentiation.

### 7. Carousel skeleton unspecified ⚠
We have 23 named skeletons in our bank. Calendar mentions "Versus Split" or "News Headline Strip" but doesn't lock which performs best per use case.

### 8. CTA stacking ❌
Some posts have 2-3 CTAs ("comment X · DM us · click link"). Top performers use ONE CTA per post.

## Data-backed hook rewrites

### DAY 1 · MON · Soft launch teaser

**Pattern to use:** Era-end declaration (proven 210K)

**Old:** "Big things tomorrow at 9 AM ET"

**Rewrite (Era-end):**
> "It is officially over. Production companies were the middlemen. We just removed them."

**Or shorter (matches X format):**
> "Production companies have 24 hours left. videfy.com goes public tomorrow."

**Why this works:** Same structural pattern as 210K post. Polarizing. Implies confidence. Generates immediate reaction (agree/disagree). Roy-Lee-tier.

---

### DAY 2 · TUE · Main launch

**Pattern to use:** Direct cost reframe (proven 126K + 312K)

**Old:** "We just made AI video production not suck"

**Rewrite (Cost reframe):**
> "Your ad agency charges $80K for this. We just shipped it for $59 a month."

**Or with year (312K pattern):**
> "You would've paid $250K for this in 2024. Today we ship it for the price of one Adobe seat."

**Why this works:** 312K-view post used identical structure. Specific dollars. Direct competitor callout. Trial conversion tested.

---

### DAY 3 · WED · Build-in-public

**Pattern to use:** Process denial trio (proven 240K)

**Old:** "How we built Videfy"

**Rewrite (Process denial trio):**
> "We didn't film it. Didn't book talent. Didn't open Premiere. 47 beta users tore it apart and rebuilt it. 6 months later — here's what shipped."

**Or shorter X version:**
> "6 months. 47 beta users. 60% rebuilt twice. Here's the receipt ↓"

**Why this works:** Process denial = 240K-view pattern. Numbers stack = build-in-public credibility. Receipts framing = X audience native.

---

### DAY 4 · THU · Generate tier

**Pattern to use:** Stop-start (proven 112K) OR conditional shame (proven 229K)

**Old:** "You don't need to sign up to use Videfy"

**Rewrite (Stop-start · TT/IG):**
> "Stop creating accounts to test AI tools. Start using one that doesn't ask."

**Rewrite (Conditional shame · X):**
> "If you've signed up for 47 AI tools this year, you're being trained, not using them."

**Why this works:** Both are proven 100K+ patterns. Stop-start has Roy Lee endorsement. Conditional shame closer to ohneis core voice (229K post).

---

### DAY 5 · FRI · Case study

**Pattern to use:** Direct cost reframe (already on the right track)

**Old:** "[Beta user] just shipped a $40K campaign in an hour"

**Sharpen (matches 126K cost-reframe pattern):**
> "Her agency quoted $40K for this campaign. She made it in 47 minutes."

**Why this works:** Already in the proven pattern family. Just specifics-tightened (47 minutes > "an hour" — odd numbers convert higher in copywriting research).

---

### DAY 6 · SAT · Recap

**Pattern to use:** Tool callout shame (136K)

**Old:** "Wk 1 by the numbers"

**Rewrite:**
> "Most launches lie about week 1. Here's our actual receipts."

**Or stop-start:**
> "Stop reading polished launch recaps. Start reading the actual numbers."

**Why this works:** Polarizing. Implies competitor critique. Sets up transparency angle.

---

### DAY 7 · SUN · Metrics thread

**Pattern to use:** All-in-one flex (205K) repurposed for transparency

**Rewrite:**
> "Week 1 receipts ↓
>
> Every view. Every signup. Every lost user. Auto-thread because we're off Sunday."

**Why this works:** "Every X · every Y · every Z" mirrors 205K-view pattern. The "off Sunday" line adds founder humanity (different from corporate auto-tweets).

**Final length target:** 650 words.

## Universal copy bans (apply across all)

### Cut these (zero in top-10 viral data)
- ❌ "We just" (overused launch tic)
- ❌ "Excited to announce"
- ❌ "After months of"
- ❌ "Big things"
- ❌ "Game-changer / disruptive / revolutionary"
- ❌ "Today's the day"

### Replace with (used in top 10)
- ✓ Specific dollars ($40K, $80K, $250K, $3K)
- ✓ Specific numbers (47 minutes, 6 weeks, 8 tools, 312K views)
- ✓ Active verbs (killed, shipped, fired, broke, rebuilt, removed)
- ✓ Direct callouts ("Everyone's still using X" / "Your agency charges Y")
- ✓ Conditional shame ("If your X still does Y...")
- ✓ Era-end ("It is officially over...")
