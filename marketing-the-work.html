1The launch plan Plan ready Launch not fired yet

What it is

A step-by-step plan for how we launch Videfy with only two people on the marketing team. It's split into 5 phases, from "getting ready" all the way to "scale up." Right now we are still in Phase 0 — Get Ready.

  • Phase 0 — Get ready (6 weeks before launch)
  • Phase 1 — Launch day
  • Phase 2 — First 4 weeks after
  • Phase 3 — Build the audience (weeks 5 to 12)
  • Phase 4 — Turn followers into customers (weeks 13 to 26)

How I built it

Benchmarked 12 SaaS launches (Descript, Runway, CapCut Pro, Opus Clip, Submagic, and more) and tagged each tactic by team size + budget required. Kept the tactics that worked for small teams. Dropped the ones that only work with big budgets. The result is a plan a 2-person team can actually finish.

2Who we talk to Locked May 13

What it is

We picked one main group of people to focus on: solo creators. These are people who make their own videos for a living. We are not chasing big agencies or huge companies right now.

  • Main group: solo creators
  • Backup group: small teams and content managers
  • Skip for now: agencies and big companies

How I built it

Started with research, not guesses. Scraped engagement data from each candidate group — creators, teams, agencies. The data showed agencies have low intent signals and enterprise sales cycles run 6+ months. Then I mined 30 top Reddit and X threads where video creators describe their pain — that became the foundation for our messaging. Built a 5-question on-site survey so future signups self-segment into the right tier instead of us guessing.

3How we explain what Videfy does Done Lead pick needed

What it is

Videfy has 4 different ways to use it. I gave each one its own marketing story so people can find the door that fits them best.

  • Claude MCP — Claude becomes your AI video producer
  • Variants — make many versions of one video fast
  • Avatar — make videos with AI people
  • Premiere extension — plug Videfy into Adobe Premiere

How I built it

Researched how SaaS products with multiple features position each entry point — Runway, Descript, Notion, CapCut Pro. The pattern: each use case gets its own door (landing page + demo + goal). Applied that pattern to Videfy's 4 avenues. Locked the line between "what Claude does today" vs "what it might do later" so marketing copy stays inside what the product can actually do right now.

4How prices show up on the site Direction locked Final approval needed

What it is

We have 5 price levels, from free to enterprise. I wrote how each one is named, what it includes, and how it's explained on the site.

  • Generate — free, no sign-up needed (front door)
  • Creator — $24/month
  • Pro — $59/month (best value)
  • Team — $39 per person/month

How I built it

Benchmarked how 6 comparable products (Runway, Descript, CapCut Pro, Adobe, Canva, DaVinci) frame price on their sites. Picked the hybrid model (monthly fee + included credits) because the data shows it converts better than credits-only or seat-only at our price point. Renamed the old beta tier names (Hobby, Step In, Grow, Scale) into clearer ones (Generate, Creator, Pro, Team) to match how the audience actually searches and talks.

7How slideshow posts work Locked May 26

What it is

Rules for the slideshow posts we put on LinkedIn and Instagram. Every slideshow has to feel like a new way of thinking — not just a tip.

  • Start with the strongest image (a face, a person, a color)
  • Backgrounds: mood-first, never a literal picture of the headline
  • No screens, no monitors, no laptops in the photos
  • Two fonts: Inter Black + Cormorant Bold Italic
  • Every reel ending: name a real Videfy feature (Variants, MCP, Avatar, etc)

How I built it

I studied the top 100 best slideshow posts in our space (creators like filatov.design, bywaviboy, prompts.ig). I copied the pattern that worked: show a paradigm shift, not a tip list. Then I built 38 slideshow posts ready to go through June 29.

8How we write (voice + hooks) Done

What it is

The biggest body of work. Rules for what every post says and how it sounds. So Videfy always sounds like Videfy.

  • 8 hook patterns — different ways to grab attention in the first line
  • Voice — kind and confident. Not angry. Sassy, not preachy.
  • "We" voice only — we always speak as the company, never as a person
  • No em dashes ever
  • No fake stats — never make up numbers
  • No "Most people..." openings (sounds like a listicle)
  • Evergreen — every post still makes sense 6 months later
  • 75% positive, 25% pain — not all complaints
  • Mix the hooks — don't start every post with "What if..."

How I built it

I wrote 30 versions of our top 30 scripts. Each version learned from what worked and what flopped. I also studied how Justin Welsh writes (a big LinkedIn creator) and mined Reddit + X for what video creators actually complain about. The result: 20+ rules anyone on the team can follow without guessing.

10How often we post (and the month-one calendar) Locked May 18 Month-one loaded

What it is

A weekly rhythm so a 2-person team can ship a lot without burning out — plus a full month of content already built and loaded into Airtable.

  • Alex does a live show + direction
  • Misha turns the live into posts
  • Maggie sends outreach messages
  • 1 live show = 16+ posts + 200+ outreach touches
  • Month-one is fully prepped in Airtable — every post, every caption, every channel, dated and assigned. Misha just publishes from the queue.

How I built it

Started with the constraint: 2 people can't post on 5 platforms every day. Picked one big weekly anchor (the live show) and built everything else to flow from it. Then built a 6-table Airtable base that holds the entire month-one schedule. Each row = one post, with its channel, caption, image link, date, and owner. Wrote 7 setup scripts (setup_misha_airtable, setup_content_views, populate_copy, populate_video_copy, swap_copy_to_caption, reseed_content, scrape_apify) so the calendar can be reseeded fast and nothing gets typed twice.
