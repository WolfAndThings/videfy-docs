1The launch plan Plan ready Launch not fired yet

What it is

A step-by-step plan for how we launch Videfy with only two people on the marketing team. It's split into 5 phases, from "getting ready" all the way to "scale up." Right now we are still in Phase 0 — Get Ready.

  • Phase 0 — Get ready (where we are now)
  • Phase 1 — Launch day
  • Phase 2 — Cold start (the first weeks after launch)
  • Phase 3 — Audience build
  • Phase 4 — Conversion + scale

How I built it

Benchmarked 12 SaaS launches (Descript, Runway, CapCut Pro, Opus Clip, Submagic, and more) and tagged each tactic by team size + budget required. Kept the tactics that worked for small teams. Dropped the ones that only work with big budgets. The result is a plan a 2-person team can actually finish.

2What we'll track + the rollout phases Structure locked

What we'll track once we launch

4 things we'll watch from day one. Actual target numbers get set at the real launch date — too far out to lock now.

  • Followers across LinkedIn · X · Instagram · YouTube · TikTok (when we get the handle)
  • Paying users — trial signups + paid conversions per tier

The rollout phases (run in order after launch)

  • Identity Flip: plant the "you're the studio now" idea. 1 reel/day, all platforms.
  • Chaos Validation: show the pain. Viewers see their problem, name it, expect Videfy to fix it.
  • Capability Demo: show what Videfy actually does. Convert curiosity into trial.
  • Social Proof + Pricing: trial → paid. Stack proof with customer micro-case-studies.
  • Scale + Affiliate Activation: open the affiliate program. Ride the audience flywheel.

How I built it

Benchmarked the phase order against comparable cold-start SaaS launches. Each phase has a single job. The order matters — skip a phase and the next one lands flat. Built the asset bank so each phase already has the posts it needs the day we move into it.

6Ways people sign up Locked

What it is

5 different doors people can walk through to start using Videfy. Each one is built for a different kind of person at a different level of trust.

  • Door 1 — Generate (free, no signup): credits-only front door. Try Videfy without an account. Easiest first step.
  • Door 2 — 14-day Pro trial (no credit card): the main signup path. Shows full value fast without payment friction.
  • Door 3 — Free Pro for Tier 1 + 3 creators: 50 free Pro accounts for Tier 1 (50K+ followers). 100 free Pro accounts for Tier 3 (1K-5K, fastest-yes group). Used to seed the launch.
  • Door 4 — Affiliate-driven signup: someone signs up through a creator's link. Creator gets 30% × 12 months commission.
  • Door 5 — Discord onboarding: warm leads from the ICP survey route into Discord, then convert from there.

How I built it

Researched conversion data on each entry point across comparable SaaS launches. Free tier alone invites parking (users who never convert). Credits-only alone scares people off. The 14-day Pro trial without credit card gave the highest trial-to-paid rate in benchmarks. Layered the 5 doors so different audience types can enter where they're most comfortable.

Rules for the slideshow posts we put on LinkedIn and Instagram. Every slideshow has to feel like a new way of thinking — not just a tip.

I studied the top 100 best slideshow posts in our space (creators like filatov.design, bywaviboy, prompts.ig). I copied the pattern that worked: show a paradigm shift, not a tip list. Then I built 38 slideshow posts ready to go through June 29.

10How we write (voice + hooks) Done

What it is

The biggest body of work. Rules for what every post says and how it sounds. So Videfy always sounds like Videfy.

  • 8 hook patterns — different ways to grab attention in the first line
  • Voice — kind and confident. Not angry. Sassy, not preachy.
  • "We" voice only — we always speak as the company, never as a person
  • No em dashes ever
  • No fake stats — never make up numbers
  • No "Most people..." openings (sounds like a listicle)
  • Evergreen — every post still makes sense 6 months later
  • 75% positive, 25% pain — not all complaints
  • Mix the hooks — don't start every post with "What if..."

How I built it

I wrote 30 versions of our top 30 scripts. Each version learned from what worked and what flopped. I also studied how Justin Welsh writes (a big LinkedIn creator) and mined Reddit + X for what video creators actually complain about. The result: 20+ rules anyone on the team can follow without guessing.

Production schedule (per live)

Full start-to-finish workflow locked. Every live runs on the same beats so the team isn't rebuilding the plane each week.

  • T-minus 7 days — title locked · build picked · outline drafted · announcement posts on LI / X / IG / beta email · Discord pinned · HubSpot form active
  • T-minus 3 days — 10 Q&A seed questions · reminder email · daily story countdown · platform sanity check
  • T-minus 24 hours — 24-hr reminder email · reposts on X + LI · Discord announcement
  • T-minus 2 hours — personal prep (eat, hydrate, kill notifications)
  • T-minus 1 hour — full team day-of block: 15 min setup · 15 min tech test · 15 min rehearsal · 15 min dry run
  • T-minus 5 min — soft launch · "starting soon" card · music bed · live ops watches join flow
  • T-zero (0:00-0:05) — open · trust block · oneliners · what audience will get
  • Walkthrough (0:05-0:30) — main build
  • Q&A + CTA close
  • Full playbook written (Zoom + Discord + HubSpot)
  • Host dashboard, audience dashboard, runbook, timeline
  • Live #1 ran today as the first Cinematic Dark walk-through

How I built it

Research showed live demos convert better than recorded explainers across SaaS launches we benchmarked. Picked it as the weekly anchor for 3 reasons (a) it's the one place where the founder + product show up in real time, (b) one live becomes 16+ smaller posts after (clip math from comparable launches), (c) the format works for a 2-person team. Built the full repeatable setup (dashboard, host HUD, runbook, timeline) so every future live is just swap content, not rebuild.
