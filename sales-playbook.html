Lane 1 · Direct sales (POC)
Direct sales · proof of value
Booked meetings with named buyers. Hands-on POC, not self-serve.

Wedge features: Storyboard · Subtitles · Premiere export · Multi-variant.

Offer: phased rollout tied to quality. Discount, but only after working with direct named people first.

Outcome: closed deal + reference logo for outbound.

Educator partnership program
People + companies who train creators / marketers on video.
Frame: partnership + relationship, not sale. Story + benefits. Garry's call: partnership program, not affiliate.

Ladder: try → teach with it → 3-month discount → lifecycle partnership.

Target: companies training groups of ~5 candidates. Channels + educators.

Outcome: recurring student pipeline + co-marketing distribution.

02 · Direct sales lane

Four meetings.
Discovery → Demo → POC → Close.

Lane 1 · Direct sales (POC)
Sales meeting flow
Mtg 1Discovery
Define their problems. Massage pain points. No pitch, no demo, no price. Map them to the ICP message map + pain-row Videfy answers. Next: book demo, send 1-pager
Mtg 2Demo
Show wedge features tied to their pain. Storyboard (for cold-start / structure pain), Multi-variant (for ad teams), Subtitles (for reformat tax), Premiere export (for editor handoff). Skip features that don't ladder to a pain they named in Mtg 1. Next: scope POC + invite to honeypot
Mtg 3POC
Hands-on with direct named people. Phased, quality-gated. Define success criteria in writing. Track time-to-first-storyboard, first export, first variant batch. Co-build one real project, not a sandbox. Next: review, lock offer terms
Mtg 4Close
Offer + discount. Discount tied to "direct named" status (early access, not public). Confirm tier, seats, contract terms, billing. Next: contract signed, kickoff date set
Open gap · blocks Mtg 4
The offer is not locked. Need package + price + discount terms before any Mtg 4 fires. Without it, POCs stall. Resolve before Phase 2 launches.
03 · Educator partnership lane

Four touches.
Relationship, not sale.

Lane 2 · Educator partnership
Educator partnership flow
Pre-req: talk to Ophir first.
Mtg 1Intro
"We know you teach video. We'd love to meet." Story + benefits. No platform pitch yet. Frame as partnership exploration, never affiliate. Next: send trial credentials + partnership 1-pager
Mtg 2Trial
They try the platform personally. Walk through Storyboard, Multi-variant, Avatar, Claude MCP. Get their honest take on what they'd actually teach with. Next: design student cohort run
Mtg 3Teach
They use Videfy with a group (~5 students). We give onboarding support + custom credits. Track student outputs as social proof. Co-publish if possible. Next: 3-month discount + partnership terms
Mtg 4Partnership
Lock partnership program terms. 3-month discount lead-in → ongoing lifecycle benefit. Co-marketing, story sharing, channel access. Not an affiliate cut. Next: signed partnership agreement, joint announcement
Naming rule (locked)
Always say "partnership program." Never say "affiliate." Drop "20%/10% affiliate" language entirely. Garry's call. Partnership = relationship + co-marketing + benefits, not transactional cut.
Open gap · blocks Mtg 4
Partnership terms not defined. 3-month discount value + lifecycle benefit + co-marketing scope all blank. Lock before Phase 3 launches.

09 · Sales rules · non-negotiable

Every meeting.
Every doc. Every deal.

Rule 01
Every meeting ends w/ a next step
+ date on calendar before they hang up
No open-ended calls. No "we'll be in touch." Confirm the next mtg or next action with a date before the call closes.
Rule 02
Partnership program. Never affiliate.
Garry's call · locked
Across every deck, DM, contract, and call. Drop "affiliate" entirely. Drop "20%/10% affiliate" rate language. Partnership = relationship + co-marketing + benefits.
Rule 03
Discount only for direct-named buyers
Not public, not self-serve
The discount is part of working hands-on with us in Phase 2 POCs. Public pricing stays full price. The deal IS the access.
Rule 04
Define problem first. Pitch second.
Mtg 1 is discovery only
No demo, no price, no deck in Mtg 1. Pull problem statement from the buyer. Match to Reddit-verbatim pain row. Then Mtg 2 demo lands on exactly their language.

Verbatim moves · use as-written

MoveUse itWhen
3-question opener "This call is not about me. This call is about you. My agenda is not important. If we walked away today and I didn't answer the three questions you came with, it would be a total waste of your time. What are those three questions?" Mtg 1 open · Discovery
Commission breath "Hey, I've got a couple kids. Do I work on commission? Yes. Do I want to sell you as much stuff as possible? That'd be great. But that stuff's not important to you. I'm not going to bring it up again." Mtg 1 · early. Removes adversarial energy.
Never "just following up" Every email needs ONE of: new information · specific question · clear next step. If none of those, don't send. Every follow-up · always
