Pricing direction memo
Pain-free pricing
for a pain-free platform.
Anonymous browse at the front (no signup until Generate is clicked). Free Personal Workspace seeded on signup. Three paid subscription tiers (Creator · Pro · Team) priced per workspace, not per seat. One Enterprise tier for everything custom. Credits live inside the workspace, allocated by Owner and Admin. Clients always review for free, no account required, unlimited at every tier. Built to serve four GTM avenues: Claude + ChatGPT MCP, the anonymous-browse funnel, Avatar, and the Adobe Premiere Extension.
The architectural spine

Account, Workspace,
Personal Workspace.

Pricing only makes sense once the account/workspace model is clear. Get this wrong and every tier collapses into confusion.

👤
Account
A user identity
A single user identity. Created via email. Can be a member of many workspaces with a different role in each. Holds zero credits and zero subscriptions on its own — those live on workspaces.
🗃
Workspace
Financial container
Owns a subscription. Owns a credit pool. Owns its projects, members, roles. One Owner per workspace. Members can be Admins, Editors, or Viewers. Workspaces are fully isolated from each other (Restricted Workspace Model).
📁
Personal Workspace
"My Projects" · auto-created
Auto-created on signup. The account holder is always the Owner. Free at baseline (promo credits seeded on signup). Can upgrade to Creator or Pro. Financially walled off from any invited workspace. Every account always has one.
Why this matters for pricing
Tier = subscription on a workspace, not on an account. One account can own multiple paid subscriptions (one per workspace owned). Personal Workspace is always free. Anonymous visitors land here the moment they sign up. Workspaces are financially independent. Credits do not pool across workspaces.
Collaboration model

Five roles.
Simple.

Five roles. That is it. Two operate at the workspace level (Owner, Admin), three operate per project (Editor, Viewer, Approver). Tier differentiation lives in how many paid roles an Owner can invite. Viewers and Approvers are always free and unlimited.

👑
Owner
Workspace-level
Pays the bill. Holds the subscription. Allocates credits. Invites members. Assigns Admins. Can delete the workspace. One per workspace.
🛠
Admin
Workspace-level
Manages members, assigns roles, manages projects, allocates credits to users and projects. Cannot purchase credits, change subscription, or delete workspace. Day-to-day operator.
✏️
Editor
Per-project · paid seat
Assigned to projects. Can create projects, edit assigned projects, generate content (against per-Editor token budget set by Owner / Admin). Counts as a paid seat.
👁
Viewer
Per-project · the wedge
View + leave comments (if Owner enables). No Videfy account required — name + password access. Free, unlimited at every paid tier. Does not consume tokens. Does not count as a seat.
✅
Approver
Per-project · sign-off + the wedge
Same access as Viewer (no Videfy account, name + password) plus the power to sign off on a version. Approval action requires email-verified one-time code → audit trail is real (who approved what, when). Locks the version as approved. Free, unlimited at every paid tier.
Why Viewer + Approver is the wedge
Every other tool taxes you per reviewer and per approver. Videfy ships both as free.
Frame.io, CapCut Business, Adobe — all charge per seat regardless of whether the seat ever generates anything, and approval workflows are usually a paid add-on on top. Owner toggles comments and approvals on or off per workspace. Five clients, twenty stakeholders, fifty internal reviewers, three approvers — none of them affect the bill. This is the single most important pricing decision in the doc.
Approver — credible without an account
Signs in with name + password (set by Owner). Approving a version sends a one-time code to the email Owner specified at invite. Approver enters the code → approval recorded with email-verified identity. Audit trail visible to Owner / Admin. v2: triggers payment milestone events for client work.
Usage

Pain-free,
predictable.

Usage is dead simple, and credits don't disappear on you.

One pool per workspace · credits roll · one bill
Each workspace has a monthly token allowance. Resets on the billing date. Credits roll over month-to-month while the subscription is active — capped at 2 months of allowance so the pool never balloons. Credits expire on cancellation or downgrade. When the rolling pool hits zero, the Owner can buy a top-up or wait for the next reset. Editors draw from the workspace pool, capped by per-Editor budget Owner / Admin sets. Viewers never touch tokens. Personal Workspace credits and paid-workspace credits do not pool.
Pricing-page line
"Your credits roll. Use them when the work shows up."
Credits you paid for don't disappear. We don't punish you for a slow week. The pool grows up to 2 months of allowance, then plateaus — predictable for you, predictable for us.
Monetization architecture

Three layers.
One coherent funnel.

①
Layer 1 — Acquisition
Anonymous browse · no signup
Anonymous browse — no signup needed to explore the homepage, Story Creation, Start from Scratch, or watch tutorials. The moment a user clicks Generate, a signup modal pops (email + password, no card). Backend gates every generation endpoint with auth, so the modal is UX while security stays server-side. First click → account → seeded Personal Workspace.
②
Layer 2 — Core recurring
Personal · Creator · Pro · Team · Enterprise
Subscription plans on workspaces. Persistent projects, organization, repeatability, collaboration, free unlimited client review, production-system value.
③
Layer 3 — Expansion
Top-ups + metered heavy actions
Top-ups for above-allowance use, metered heavy actions, future premium / advanced generation. Belongs to subscribed workspaces.
The story in one line
Anonymous browse gets users in. Personal Workspace + paid subscriptions hold recurring workflow value. Top-ups monetize spikes. Free Viewers are the wedge that makes the pricing different from every competitor.
Free value layer — delivered through content, not product
Videfy already has a permanent free product surface (anonymous browse + Personal Workspace). On top of that, free value is delivered through content: tutorials, tips, workflow breakdowns across IG, TikTok, LinkedIn, X, YouTube — with Discord as the most active surface for frameworks, prompts, early access, community feedback.
The recommendation

Hybrid wins,
and it is not close.

Three pricing patterns are live: subscription + included usage (Runway, Descript, CapCut Pro), per-seat business pricing (Adobe, Canva, Descript Business), one-time license (DaVinci Resolve). For a workflow product with variable-cost AI actions, the strongest pattern is hybrid — predictable subscription plus metered usage for heavy actions.

✓
Why hybrid wins
Why we pick this
  • Easier to understand than credits-only
  • Better cost control than subscription-only
  • Familiar — Descript, Runway, CapCut train the pattern
  • Supports both creator and team buyers
  • Keeps the homepage clean (no credit jargon)
✕
Why not per-seat scaling at public tiers
Save it for Enterprise
Per-seat scaling forces every contributor — even a junior editor or a client reviewer — onto a paid line item. Where competitors get expensive and confusing. Videfy keeps seats fixed per tier and pushes seat overages to Enterprise. Public tiers stay flat-priced.
✕
Why not credits-only as primary
Top-up surface only
  • Too abstract for first-time buyers
  • Feels risky and hard to budget
  • Hard to communicate value on homepage
  • Better as internal meter than top-level story

Why Skills matter for pricing
Skills compound platform value over time. Videfy doesn't have to build every vertical — the community does. Network effect: more skills → more reasons to use Videfy → more skill creators. The marketplace is a viral discovery surface and a long-term moat.
Rules — pricing implication
Project-level rules ship at every paid tier. Workspace-level rules (apply to all projects in a workspace) ship Creator and up. Owner / Admin lock vs Editor-proposed rules = Team and up. Rules are not metered — they are configuration, not generation.

Restricted Workspace Model

Privacy
by default.

Not a setting. The default. Members see only projects they are explicitly assigned to. Members do not see the full project list, do not see other members, do not see the workspace's total credit pool. Owner and Admin see everything. Viewers see only the project they were invited to.

🛡
Client data isolation
Agency unlock
Agencies put many clients in one workspace without cross-contamination.
🧱
Internal team segmentation
Need-to-know
Junior editors don't see the strategy work. Freelancers don't see the client roster.
💰
Credit spending control
Owner / Admin only
Only Owner / Admin see the pool. Editors see only their allocation.
Competitive positioning
Frame.io, CapCut Business, Adobe team plans do not enforce this by default. This belongs on the pricing page and the agency landing page.

How they feed each other

Guardrails

What not to do
this launch cycle.

1
Don't use credits-only as primary monetization
Credits are a top-up surface for spikes — not the main plan.
2
No traditional unlimited free tier inside paid product
Anonymous browse + Personal Workspace replace "free" without parking users on zero revenue. Personal gives the always-on free home for an account.
3
Don't let Personal cannibalize Creator
Editor invites, Adobe extension, larger pools, Team features stay behind their respective walls.
4
No per-seat scaling at public tiers
Public tiers flat per workspace. Seat overages = Enterprise only.
5
Don't over-feature Enterprise on day one
Sales-led capture line and pressure-relief valve for tier seat caps.
6
Don't make the paid pricing page explain credits
Credits stay an internal meter at paid tiers.
7
Don't undercut Pro with too much in Creator
Creator should be enough to ship, not enough to never upgrade.
8
Don't match Adobe's price ladder
Different positioning, buyer, story.
9
Don't charge for Viewers or Approvers, ever
This is the wedge. Even Enterprise should not turn either into a per-seat line item. The line that makes Videfy structurally different from Frame.io / CapCut Business / Adobe — they charge for reviewers AND for approval workflows. Videfy ships both as free.
10
Don't pool credits across workspaces
Each workspace is its own financial container — by PRD design and by pricing clarity.
